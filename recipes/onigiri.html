Onigiri Recipe

Description

Stuffed with a variety of fillings and flavors, these rice balls make an ideal quick snack and are a fun alternative to sandwiches for lunch. In this recipe, you’ll learn how to make onigiri (musubi) using the common ingredients for rice balls in Japan.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • Kosher/Sea Salt
  • 4 sheets nori, cut into halves or thirds
  • Filling of choice (salted salmon, okaka, tuna mayo, umeboshi, seasoned kombu
  • Toasted Sesame Seeds to garnish

Instructions

  1. First, wet both of your hands with water in order to prevent the rice from sticking to your hands.
  2. Then put some salt in your hands and rub to spread all around your palms. How much salt? I dip 3 finger tips in kosher salt shown in the picture below. If you are using table salt, use half amount as it’s saltier than kosher salt.
  3. Scoop out a handful of warm rice (about ⅓ cup) into one hand. Create a small well (indentation) in the center of the rice. Put one kind of filling (about 1-2 tsp) inside. Then mold the rice with your hands around the well to cover your filling completely.
  4. Press the rice around the filling to gently form the rice into a triangle. I use three fingers (thumb, index finger, middle finger) to make a triangle corner. Your hands should be just firm enough so the onigiri doesn’t fall apart. You don’t want to squeeze the rice too tight.
  5. Wrap the onigiri with nori seaweed.
  6. Place a little bit of each filling on top of onigiri so we know which kind it is.
